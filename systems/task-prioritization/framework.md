# Task Prioritization Framework

## How I Decide What to Work On

To avoid confusion, I use a simple decision system based on two factors:

- Importance
- Urgency

---

## Step 1: List All Tasks

I start by listing all tasks for the day.

---

## Step 2: Categorize Tasks

I divide tasks into 4 categories:

### 1. Important + Urgent
- Tasks with deadlines
- Tasks that impact outcomes directly

👉 Action: Do these first

---

### 2. Important + Not Urgent
- Long-term work
- Planning, improvement tasks

👉 Action: Schedule time for these

---

### 3. Not Important + Urgent
- Interruptions
- Some emails or requests

👉 Action: Minimize or delegate if possible

---

### 4. Not Important + Not Urgent
- Low-value tasks

👉 Action: Avoid or eliminate

---

## Step 3: Focus on Top 2–3 Tasks

Instead of trying to do everything, I focus on:

- 2–3 important tasks for the day

---

## Key Learning

When I use this framework:

- I make better decisions  
- I avoid wasting time on low-value work  
- I feel more in control of my work
